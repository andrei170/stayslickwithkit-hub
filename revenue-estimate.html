Most Likely Read

$15-25K/mo, $180-300K/yr.

Lean toward the lower end of base. The Trustpilot review count (17 in 2+ years) and the low public site traffic (~30/mo per StatShow, likely undercounted but directional) both suggest a smaller customer pool than the audience size implies. He has demand in his audience. He doesn't have plumbing converting it.

Most Uncertain Assumptions (in order of risk)

  1. Whether the membership exists at all. No public Skool found. App.slickwithkit.com runs on Squarespace Member Areas. If there's no recurring tier, knock $1-8K/mo off every scenario.
  2. Course flow rate. 17 Trustpilot reviews in 2+ years implies far fewer than 20 buyers/mo. Could be 3-8. If you assume 5/mo, course revenue is ~$3K/mo not $12K.
  3. Held-call close rate. Could be unusually high (small qualified pool) or unusually low (broken funnel). User's color on the call quality will resolve this fast.
  4. Trustpilot under-collection. Most coaches don't ask for Trustpilot reviews. Real customer count could be 3-5x the review volume. Don't read the count as a hard ceiling, only as a signal.

Implication for the Pitch

This is a fixable mid-sized creator, not a huge whale.

  • He can absolutely afford a $5-10K/mo retainer.
  • A $15-25K one-time install (VSL + funnel + content shift) is a no-brainer if you can show 30-50% revenue lift in 90 days.
  • The math works on impact alone: a single extra $4K close per month covers a $5K retainer twice over.

Don't oversell. The pitch is "you have a $200K business with $400-500K mechanics already in place — you just need the plumbing." That's an honest read. It's also exactly the right size to close.

Pitch Math (for the call, sanitised version)

If asked "what would this do for me," your honest range:

"Conservatively, fixing the VSL gap and the cancellation leak alone tends to add 30-50% to held-call volume and 1-2 closes a month at the $4K tier. That's $4-8K/mo in net new revenue from the same top-of-funnel you already have. The content shift is a 90-day compound on top of that. We're not talking 10x — we're talking the difference between a $200K year and a $300K+ year, with the same audience."

That framing gives him a number, ties it to mechanics he can verify, and doesn't promise the world.
