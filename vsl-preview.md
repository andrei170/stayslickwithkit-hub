# VSL Preview — Kit's $4K 1:1 Coaching Funnel

> Short preview, not a full long-form script. ~3-5 min run time. Designed to slot above the application form on a dedicated `/1-1-vsl` page. Matches Kit's voice, references his real proof points, hooks the wealthier-demo target.

**Target buyer:** Professional woman, 30-45, career-built (founder / senior corporate / surgeon / lawyer), single 12+ months, frustrated with the dating pool not matching the rest of her life. Can write a £4K cheque without flinching.

**Offer being pitched in the VSL:** Kit's 1:1 coaching package ($4K, currently — see `funnel-blueprint.md` for repricing).

**Tone:** Direct, warm, theatre-trained delivery. Kit's actual voice. No motivational coach energy. He's the expert, not the cheerleader.

---

## Structure (Short Preview Outline)

### Hook — 0:00 to 0:15

> "If you're a woman who's built a career, runs a company, or makes decisions other people listen to — and you still can't figure out why your dating life looks like the worst version of someone else's — you're not the problem. The men you're meeting are."

Cuts through immediately. Validates the smart audience. Names the pain.

### Pattern Interrupt — 0:15 to 0:35

> "I'm Kit. I'm an actor. I've been on the West End. I've voiced characters in *Baldur's Gate*. None of that is why you're going to listen to me. You're going to listen to me because for the last few years, I've worked privately with women who run companies, lead teams, manage practices — and one by one, I've watched them go from 'I can't find a man who can keep up' to engaged, married, in real partnerships."

Credibility without ego. Names his actual proof points. Reframes acting as a curiosity, not a credential.

### Problem agitation — 0:35 to 1:15

> "Here's what I see, almost every time. You're successful. You've optimised every other part of your life. Career — sorted. Health — sorted. Friendships — sorted. Dating? Total chaos. Because the same things that made you a high performer in your career — the standards, the directness, the refusal to settle — they're working AGAINST you in dating, when nobody taught you the rules.
>
> And the dating advice you've found online? Most of it is for the 24-year-old who's just been ghosted for the third time this month. Not for you. You don't need 'pick yourself up after a breakup.' You need 'how do I actually find a man who's at my level.' Different problem. Different fix."

Names the demo specifically. Validates the frustration. Separates them from the entry-level audience.

### Solution + Mechanism — 1:15 to 2:15

> "What I do isn't 'date coaching' the way you've seen it before. It's three things, and I do them with you over 12 weeks, one-on-one.
>
> One — we map your actual standards. Not the negotiated-down version you've been settling for. The real ones. And we make you fluent in them, so you can spot a misalignment in 10 minutes, not 10 months.
>
> Two — we rebuild how you show up. Most successful women have been told their career is 'intimidating' to men. That's nonsense. The successful version of you is exactly what the right kind of man is looking for. We show you how to be that, fully, while still pulling him toward you instead of pushing him away.
>
> Three — we install a real screening process. Most women audition for men. We're going to flip that. By the end of week 4 you'll be running every man you meet through a vetting filter that takes the guesswork out, fast.
>
> No mass course. No group chat. Just me, you, and 12 weeks of one-to-one work."

Names the three pillars. Doesn't oversell. Sounds like an actual coach, not a marketer.

### Proof — 2:15 to 3:00

> "I'm not going to throw 50 testimonials at you. Here's the one I want you to see.
>
> [Insert 30-second client clip — ideally a woman 35-45 with a career, naming a specific outcome with a real time frame.]
>
> She came to me in [month]. By [month + X], she was engaged. Same thing happened with [name and outcome]. And [name and outcome]. The pattern repeats."

If clips don't exist yet, frame as: "I'm not going to flood you with testimonials. The Trustpilot's there if you want them. What I'd rather do is show you the work itself."

### CTA + Friction Reduction — 3:00 to 3:45

> "If this sounds like you, here's what I want you to do. Below this video is a short application — takes 4 minutes. I read every one personally. If we're a fit, we'll book a 30-minute call this week or next. Not a sales call. A real conversation about what you're actually looking for and whether what I do is the right vehicle for it.
>
> If we're not a fit, I'll tell you, and I'll point you toward someone who is. I'd rather lose your application than waste your time.
>
> Application's below. See you on the other side."

Direct. Removes "is this a sales call" anxiety. Permission to be honest if it's not a fit. Wealthy buyers respond to that, not pressure.

### Sign-off (5-7 sec, optional)

> "I'm Kit. Let's get you sorted."

Leans on his "let's get you sorted" UK-direct voice without overdoing it.

## Production Notes

- **Length target:** 3:30 to 4:30. Short. Wealthy buyers don't watch 12-minute VSLs unless the first 30 seconds earned it.
- **Set:** matching the photoshoot upgrade in `content-strategy.md`. Mayfair restaurant interior, hotel lobby, library, or studio with warm cinematic lighting. Not his bedroom.
- **Wardrobe:** tailored. Suit jacket open, no tie. Theatre-trained presence shows when he's framed properly.
- **Camera:** single 4K, locked off, shallow depth of field. No jump cuts. Continuous take with cinematic B-roll over the proof section.
- **Music:** soft cinematic underscore at -28 dB. Not motivational. More "Sky Atlantic drama opener" than "YouTube ad."
- **Captions:** burned-in lower third for the hook (first 15 sec) only. After that, no captions.

## Page Layout (where the VSL lives)

`/1-1-vsl` page structure:

1. Hero — VSL embedded above the fold
2. Headline below VSL (matches the hook)
3. 3 trust bars: "12-week 1:1," "Limited spots," "Application required"
4. Embedded application form directly below (no second page jump)
5. FAQ accordion (5-7 questions, see below)
6. Closing line + form CTA repeated

### FAQ for the page (anticipates objections)

- "How is this different from a course?"
- "How much time per week?"
- "What if I'm not based in London?"
- "What's the price?" (don't show the number on the page; have the application form route to a brief Calendly call where price is discussed)
- "What if I've worked with a coach before and it didn't help?"
- "What's your refund / completion policy?"
- "Why do I have to apply?"
