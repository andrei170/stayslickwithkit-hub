# Bottleneck Analysis — Kit Esuruoso

> Three bottlenecks, ranked by revenue impact. Each one is observable, fixable, and has a 90-day ceiling. The order matters.

---

## Snapshot

- ~80-150K cross-platform reach
- ~$15-25K/mo most-likely revenue (see `revenue-estimate.md`)
- 9 sales calls/month booked, 4 cancelled (5 held)
- 17 Trustpilot reviews in 2+ years — demand exists, plumbing leaks
- VSL exists for the $500 course, NOT for the $4,000 1:1
- Application form on the 1:1 has no calendar handoff (per user — verify live)
- Audience converts on "why he won't text me back" hooks while the offer is "find a high-quality man fast"

The diagnosis writes itself: a creator with strong demand-generation, weak conversion mechanics, and a demo mismatch between hook and offer.

---

## Bottleneck #1 — No VSL on the $4K Offer

**The asymmetry:** The $500 product has a VSL (`/cym-vsl`). The $4,000 product has a raw form. The funnel mechanics are inversely correlated with offer price.

**Why it's costing him:**

- High-ticket buyers need to feel certainty before they fill out a $4K application form. A VSL is the cheapest way to deliver that certainty at scale.
- Cold prospects who land on the 1:1 page get zero pre-frame, zero proof, zero risk reversal — they either fill the form on raw conviction or bounce.
- Conversion rate from page view → application is almost certainly under 1%. With a VSL it should be 3-7%.

**The fix:**

- Short VSL (3-5 min, see `vsl-preview.md`) on a dedicated `/1-1-vsl` page
- Hits BEFORE the application form, embedded directly above it
- Form auto-redirects to Calendly on submit
- Confirmation page above the fold with "what happens next"

**Estimated impact:**

- Page → application rate: 1% → 4% conservatively
- Application → call booked: same (assumed)
- Held call rate: 56% (5/9) → 75%+ once form/calendar is fixed (see Bottleneck #2)
- Net effect: 1-2 extra closes/month at $4K = **$4-8K/mo additional revenue**

**Why this is #1:** Highest absolute revenue impact, fastest install (2-3 weeks), zero dependency on him changing anything else.

## Bottleneck #2 — Sales Process / Form-to-Nothing on the Highest-Value Step

**The leak:** 4 of 9 calls cancelled last month. That's 44%.

**Why it's not a setter problem:**

A setter problem looks like: calls happen, the setter qualifies poorly, the close rate is bad. That's not what's happening here.

A calendar problem looks like: form submits → time passes → someone reaches out → prospect has cooled → "yeah let me get back to you" → cancellation.

The user's report ("Typeform, no automated call booking") matches the calendar problem exactly.

**Why high-ticket dating prospects cool fast:**

- Decision is emotional. The window between "I'm filling this out" and "I'm on a call" is where doubt creeps in.
- Without a calendar redirect, the prospect goes from peak intent to passive waiting.
- A setter reaching out 24 hours later restarts the persuasion process from scratch.

**The fix (three layers):**

1. **Form → Calendar in one click.** Application form ends with embedded Calendly slot picker. Submit → time confirmed → all in 90 seconds.
2. **Pre-call nurture sequence:**
   - T-0: Confirmation email + 60-90s personal video ("congrats, here's what we'll cover")
   - T-1 day: Case study email (real client, real timeline, real result)
   - T-morning: SMS reminder ("see you at 3pm — link is here")
3. **Setter playbook (if he keeps a setter):** see `dm-setting-playbook.md` for the full version. Key rules: no pitching in DMs, single job is calendar protection, three qualifying questions.

**Estimated impact:**

- Cancel rate 44% → 15-20%
- Held calls: 5/9 → 7-8/9
- Same close rate, +50-60% revenue per month from the same top-of-funnel volume
- Net effect: **$3-6K/mo additional revenue**

**Why this is #2:** Fast install (1-2 weeks). Direct measurable impact. Doesn't depend on his content changing. Compounds on top of Bottleneck #1.

## Bottleneck #3 — Demo Mismatch Between Hook and Offer

**The contradiction:** His top-performing reels are "why he ghosted me" and "why he won't text back." His offer is a $4K package for "professional women 25+ who want a high-quality relationship."

These attract two different people.

**Who his current content attracts:**

Frustrated women 22-32, single 6-18 months, dealing with situationships, app fatigue, low-grade dating chaos. Entry-level pain points. They become great $50/mo membership buyers and decent $597 course buyers.

**Who his $4K offer needs:**

Career-built women 30-45, founders, lawyers, doctors, ex-corporate, who can write a £4K cheque without flinching. Their pain isn't "he ghosted me." Their pain is "I run a company and I can't find a man who can match it."

**Why this is a slow-burn lever, not a fast one:**

- 60-90 day pickup minimum
- Requires content production shift, not just funnel mechanics
- Compound effect on the next quarter, not this month
- BUT — it's the lever that fills the funnel with $4K buyers indefinitely. Without it, every funnel improvement plateaus at the demo ceiling.

**The fix (without burning his viral engine):**

1. **Keep the punchy provocative reels.** They drive top-of-funnel reach. Don't kill what's working.
2. **Add a second content track for 30-45 professional women.** New hook bank (see `content-strategy.md` for full list). Examples:
   - "Why high-earning women keep dating men who can't keep up"
   - "How to date when your career is bigger than the men around you"
   - "I help women who run companies date with the standards they bring to work"
3. **Podcast guest pivot.** Stop interviewing same-tier creators. Book female founders, female VCs, c-suite women. They become content AND become buyers.
4. **Photoshoot upgrade.** He's photogenic and theatre-trained. A luxury-feel content shoot (Mayfair, suit, tailored aesthetic) reskins the brand for wealthier buyers in a weekend.
5. **Drop "Hitch" framing.** It's young/cute. Replace with "I work with women whose dating lives don't match the rest of their lives."

**Estimated impact:**

- Month 1-2: minimal (content production lead time)
- Month 3: 20-30% of new sign-ups in the higher-tier demo
- Month 6: full demo shift, average buyer LTV up 30-50%
- Net effect (90-day): **$2-4K/mo additional revenue from the same audience size**, with much higher ceiling

**Why this is #3:** Slowest payback, but the only one that addresses the underlying constraint. Without it, the funnel improvements compound on the wrong audience.

## Secondary Bottlenecks (Not in the Pitch — Save for the Build Phase)

These are real, but you don't lead with them on the call. They're fix-it-while-you're-in-there items.

1. **No lead magnet / quiz funnel.** He has 80-150K reach and almost no email list. A "What's your attachment style?" quiz would convert 10-25% of viewers to email subs. List grows by 1-2K/month from existing reach.
2. **No retargeting pixel installed cleanly.** Squarespace makes pixel install fiddly. Probably half-broken or missing. Warm traffic walks away forever.
3. **Bio link goes to homepage, not segmented.** No intent routing. Should be: free guide / podcast / course / 1:1 — separate paths.
4. **High ticket is underpriced.** $4K → $6-8K once VSL is in place and case studies stack. Pure margin expansion.
5. **17 Trustpilot reviews in 2+ years** = he's not asking. Add a post-purchase review request. Brand credibility scales.
6. **No paid ads.** Once organic funnel is plumbed, add Meta to scale predictable lead flow.
7. **"Featured in BBC" framing in bio is weak.** Replace with verifiable media wins (real podcast appearances, press features) once the credibility stack grows.
